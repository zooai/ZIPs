A Guide to Breeding and Hatching in ZOO Labs [/H]
As the non-fungible token (NFT) market continues to grow, ZOO Labs offers collectors an opportunity to diversify their digital assets with unique NFT animals. This article explores the process of breeding and hatching NFT animal eggs, providing insights into how collectors can expand their collections and potentially increase the value of their investments.

[H2] 

Breeding Basics: Understanding the Costs and Rewards [/H]
In ZOO Labs' ecosystem, breeding requires two adult NFT animals of the same species but can be of any generation. The cost of breeding is determined by the original egg price (OEP), which varies based on the rarity of the animal. For instance, a Sublime egg costs 1 ETH, a Rare egg is priced at 0.5 ETH, and an Endangered egg is valued at 0.05 ETH.
Upon successful breeding, an egg is minted and can be hatched for free or sold on the marketplace. Hatching yields a baby animal that can be grown into a teen by locking in collateral, a process known as feeding. This step not only increases the animal's value but can also earn collectors a boost for each feed.

[H2] 

Progressing through Growth Stages [/H]
To advance a baby animal to the teen stage, collectors must lock in collateral equal to the OEP. Transforming a teen into an adult requires collateral equivalent to 2x the OEP. Breeding fees, which amount to 3x the OEP, are directed to the decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) rather than staking collateral.

[H2] 

Generational Limits and Breeding Privileges [/H]
Each egg generation has a limit of one baby, one teen, and one adult that can be minted. Adults with breeding privileges can breed with another adult of the same species to mint a subsequent egg generation. However, there are restrictions on the number of generations allowed from the initial drop: the first generation can breed seven times, the second generation six times, and so on, until the seventh generation, which has no breeding privileges.
It is crucial to note that adult animals can be sold with or without remaining breeding privileges. If a first-generation animal is bred only twice and burned after 20 days, no further offspring can be produced from that animal.

Breeding Rules and Allowances: [/H]
Animals can breed with the same species and generation.
The breeding privileges are determined by the generation of the animal.
First-generation adult animals have 7 breeding allowances.
Each subsequent generation has one less breeding allowance than the previous generation.
Animals can only breed with another adult of the same species and generation if both have breeding privileges remaining.

The rarity category of the animal determines its daily allowance based on age:
* 

Sublime Animals: Baby - 1 $ZOO, Teen - 2 $ZOO, Adult - 3 $ZOO
* 

Rare Animals: Baby - 0.25 $ZOO, Teen - 0.5 $ZOO, Adult - 1 $ZOO
* 

Endangered Animals: Baby - 0.05 $ZOO, Teen - 0.1 $ZOO, Adult -0.2 $ZOO
Remember, breeding privileges are based on the generation of the animal, while the rarity category determines the daily allowance based on the age of the animal.
